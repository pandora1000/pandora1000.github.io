What is bespoke?

If there is any hype, it is purely a very small and very modern phenomenon. Interestingly enough before mass manufacturing, off-the-peg wasn't nearly as commonplace as today. A man walked into a shop, expected to have his measurements taken and sent to the factory and a few weeks later had his first fitting for the garment he ordered. The factory would cut and assemble the garment, using a proper canvas (fusible interlining wasn't invented until 1969!) and mainly from all natural materials. Not exactly bespoke but the principle stands. Those garments would fetch 200-300 pounds now. More hype, more competition lower prices...
