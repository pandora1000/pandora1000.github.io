Why I Really Really Really Love VIM!

There has never been a "hype" towards the bespoke industry. Hype tends to follow fast fashion, adapting towards the monsoon cycle and summer seasons, sticking around till winter... before the whole design board has to create something new and piles of unsold or unwanted clothes pile up in department stores, "falling" out of season, "falling" out of fashion.

If there is any hype, it is purely a very small and very modern phenomenon. Mass-manufacturing with assembly lines and rows upon rows of machinery wasn't so commonplace in the past... A man walked into a shop, expecting to have his measurements taken and sent of to a factory. Which would cut and assemble the garment together, mainly by hand and with canvas (fusible interlining wasn't invented until) and natural materials! All this being said, a bespoke garment is more long-lasting

Bespoke attracts three sorts of people. The person with an unusual body. The person who is attracted to quality, craftsmanship and tradition. In the past, off-the-peg wasn't a thing. You went in to get measured in which after an assistant would send of said measurements to the factory.
